    Sweet Baby Exposed

    TheMindIsWasted • 5/21/2025 •

    In recent months, the name Sweet Baby Inc. has moved from industry whisper to full-blown controversy in the gaming and pop culture spheres. The Montreal-based narrative consulting firm has worked with high-profile developers such as Santa Monica Studio, Ubisoft, and Insomniac Games. But their growing influence has now become the center of a heated online debate—one that involves allegations of political engineering, media manipulation, and even government involvement.

    YouTuber SmashJT and commentator Master of TDS have been at the forefront of pushing the idea that Sweet Baby Inc may not just be an inclusion consultancy, but potentially part of a coordinated “psyop” (psychological operation) with ties to government interests.

    Let’s break down the claims, the evidence, and the reaction.

    What Is Sweet Baby Inc?

    Sweet Baby Inc. describes itself as a narrative and writing consultancy that specializes in making stories more inclusive and representative of diverse perspectives. They assist developers in creating characters and worlds that reflect modern sensibilities, particularly focusing on marginalized groups. (AKA Forced DEI Injection)

    Their portfolio includes work on games like Spider-Man: Miles Morales, God of War: Ragnarök, and Horizon Forbidden West—titles praised by critics but sometimes scrutinized by fans who feel traditional storytelling has been sidelined in favor of overt political messaging.

    The Government Psyop Theory

    SmashJT and Master of TDS have raised alarms over what they view as an "unusual concentration of ideological control" over storytelling in games. Their core argument rests on the following points:

    1. Rapid Market Saturation - Despite being a relatively new company (founded in 2018), Sweet Baby Inc has secured major contracts with top-tier studios. Critics question how such quick success was possible without powerful backing.
    2. Ideological Uniformity - SmashJT points out that games worked on by Sweet Baby Inc often carry similar thematic elements: strong emphasis on identity politics, diversity checklists, and reimagined traditional archetypes. He argues this may be less about organic creativity and more about coordinated messaging.
    3. Funding and Origins - Master of TDS has drawn attention to potential links between Sweet Baby Inc and Canadian media grants, including those meant for promoting diversity and inclusion in digital content. While legal and public, some critics suggest this could hint at state-sponsored influence in entertainment media.
    4. Narrative Engineering - Both commentators suggest that Sweet Baby Inc may function as a "soft power" arm, influencing cultural output in a way that aligns with Western liberal government priorities, particularly in Canada and the U.S.

    Is There Proof?

    To date, there is no concrete evidence linking Sweet Baby Inc to any government-run psychological operation. The connections made are largely circumstantial or speculative, based on grant records, content themes, and insider speculation.

    In a recent video, SmashJT clarified, “I’m not saying there’s a smoking gun. But when the same names and ideas keep popping up across the biggest games—when it all starts sounding the same—you start asking questions.”

    Industry Response

    Many in the gaming industry have pushed back on these claims. Developers who’ve worked with Sweet Baby Inc describe the team as collaborative and passionate about storytelling.

    “They’re not forcing anything,” said one narrative lead who worked on a AAA title. “They ask questions like, ‘What story are you trying to tell?’ and help flesh that out in more inclusive ways. That’s it.”

    Others argue the psyop theory is a thinly veiled attack on progressive storytelling, designed to drum up controversy in an already polarized online space.

    Cultural War, Clicks, or Cause for Concern?

    The discussion around Sweet Baby Inc speaks to broader tensions in the gaming community. As developers strive to evolve the medium and include wider perspectives, some fans feel alienated or suspicious of top-down ideological shifts.

    While SmashJT and Master of TDS stop short of definitive accusations, their commentary has ignited debate about authenticity in game narratives and the power of external consultants in shaping our digital entertainment.

    Final Thoughts

    Whether Sweet Baby Inc is a harmless diversity consultancy, an influential cultural gatekeeper, or something more conspiratorial remains a topic of fierce debate. What’s clear is that the intersection of storytelling, ideology, and influence in the gaming industry is more contentious than ever.

    As the conversation continues, players, developers, and critics alike must navigate the fine line between thoughtful inclusion and forced messaging—and ask who’s really behind the stories we’re told.
